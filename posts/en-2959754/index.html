"Cinematic Marvel Universe» ( eng.  "The Cinematic Marvel Universe» ; abbr. CME ) - a fictional universe , the American media franchise , a series of superhero films based on the comic book company Marvel and developed film company Marvel Studios . The universe was created by combining several films and TV shows with common actors, characters and events into a common storyline.

At the moment, twenty-three films have been released . The films Iron Man (2008), The Incredible Hulk (2008), Iron Man 2 (2010), Thor (2011), Captain America: The First Avenger (2011) and Avengers (2012) are combined into the first phase . Creation

In 2005, Variety magazine announced that Marvel Studios would start producing its own films and distributing them through Paramount Pictures . Previously, the studio acted only as a co-producer in the film adaptations of the characters she invented (the most famous is the seven-year contract with 20th Century Fox ). The proceeds from these transactions, the management of Marvel decides to invest in its own generalized franchise , the creation process of which will be completely under the control of the studio. For this purpose, a revolving loan agreement was concluded for a period of 7 years with Merrill Lynch in the amount of $ 525 million [1] .

“It is both scary and fun. This is something that no one has ever done before, and it is thanks to this spirit of novelty that everyone takes this approach with enthusiasm. Other directors are not used to attracting already engaged actors from other films, to make bindings to certain storylines or places, but I think ... everyone eventually agreed to this and thought it was fun. Mainly because we invariably continue to assert: the film we are currently working on is above all. All our “bundles” are all fun and will also be very important if you want to. If fans want to look further and see a deeper connection - she will be there. There are several obvious “bundles” that, we hope, can be followed by the main audience. But ... the reasonaccording to which all the directors agreed to take part in this venture - that their films must be competitive in themselves. They need a fresh look, a unique tone, and the fact that they can be interconnected if the viewer wants to follow these tracks from bread crumbs is a bonus. ”

Cultural Influence

DC Entertainment and Warner Bros. Pictures

In October 2012, after a legal victory over Joe Schuster’s estate for Superman rights , Warner Bros. Pictures announced that DC Comics superheroes such as Batman, Superman, Wonder Woman, and other characters can finally come together on the big screen in the movie Justice League. It was assumed that the company would take the opposite approach to Marvel, releasing separate films for the characters after they appear in the combined film [105] . The release of Man from Steel in 2013 was supposed to be the beginning of a new shared universe for DC, “laying the foundation for the future release of films based on DC Comics” [106]. In 2014 Warner Bros. announced the release of films similar to Disney and Marvel, approving the dates of the films several years in advance [107] . In the same year, DC CEO Jeff Jones stated that there would be no crossover between the Strela and Flash television series and the DC cinematic universe [108] , later explaining that “we look at it as a multiverse . We have our television universe and our movie universe, but they all coexist. For us, creatively, it's about letting everyone make a better product, tell a better story, make a better world. ... It's just a different approach. " [109] .

Mentioning the first crossover of the DC universe, Batman vs. Superman: At the Dawn of Justice , in the context of KVM success reasons, Todd van der Werff noted: “Even those who have consistent directors like Christopher Nolan in the Dark Knight trilogy are actually trying to tell not long, but rather episodic stories, which may add up to the whole ... These franchises turn pale in comparison with the MCU when it comes to the complexity of the narrative, mostly telling the same story in films, with one set of characters. .. Something in the Civil War“, Especially compared to the much more awkward Batman vs. Superman, made me realize that Marvel is better at making big-screen TVs than anyone else ... Their innovation is to take the idea of ​​how superhero comics are structured, and present it in the style of a television show ... Marvel always keeps the whole in sight, sometimes to the detriment of parts. That's why other superhero movies should not try to play the Marvel mega-franchise ... That explains why the recent efforts of other superhero movie makers are so far from Marvel, though Warner Bros. and produces more traditional films. Of course, Warner Bros. there is Zach Snyder working on many films ... But Snyder and Singer are primarily immersed in the cinema and in the way the movie stories were always told. " [110] .

After a low-key BvS reception by critics and fans in May 2016, Warner Bros. attempted to “correct the course” and instructed Jeff Jones and CEO John Berg to co-manage “genre streams” in an attempt to combine disparate elements of DC films and repeat Kevin Feigy’s success in KVM. As part of their new responsibilities, Berg and Jones were appointed producers of the Justice League film, in addition to Jones participating in other DC projects, such as the suicide squad post-production and the Batman solo movie [111] . After successful release " Wonder Woman"In June 2017, DC decided to start challenging the general nature of its films, and DC Entertainment President Diane Nelson said:" Our goal is to definitely move forward using continuity to make sure no plot contradicts the rest and doesn't make sense, but we we don’t insist on a common storyline for all the pictures or on their interconnections in the current universe ... Moving forward, you will see that the universal DC film is the universe, but one that comes from the heart of the director who creates it. "In addition, DC began focusing on film, "completely separated from the rest, completely out of the cinematic universe", the first of which - a solo picture of supervillain Joker . [112] .

20th Century Fox

In November 2012, 20th Century Fox announced plans to create its own shared universe, composed of Marvel characters, in which it has the rights to include the Fantastic Four and X-Men, while hiring Mark Millar as a controlling producer. Millar said: “Fox thinks:“ We are sitting on some really amazing things here. There is another side to the Marvel Universe. Let's try and get some cohesion. " So they made me think about it. To meet with writers and directors, to suggest new ways, we could take this material and new properties that could break out of it ” [113] . " X-Men: Days of Future Past”, Released in 2014, was Fox’s first step toward expanding their stable Marvel properties and creating this universe [114] before releasing the Fantastic Four reboot movie next year [115] . However, in May 2014, the screenwriter of Days of Future Past and Fantastic Four Simon Kinberg stated that the last film would not be held in the same universe as the X-Men films, explaining that “none of the X-Men films recognize this concept of their kind of superhero - Fantastic Four. And the Fantastic Four is gaining strength, so for them to live in a world dominated by mutants, it is quite difficult, because you look like: “Oh, you're just a mutant.” For example: “What is so fantastic about you?” ... they live in different universes ” [115] . In July 2015, X-Men director Brian Singer said that there is still potential for a crossover between the X-Men and Fantastic Four franchises if the reaction to the Fantastic Four and X-Men Apocalypse justifies [116] .

In 2013, Sarik Andreasyan had an idea to make his own film about Russian superheroes , inspired by the film “ Avengers ” [128] , and the studio “ Enjoy Movies ” planned on the basis of this film to create its own franchise in order to add new superheroes with each new film in the future [129] . Universal Studios , also inspired by the success of Cinematographic Marvel universe, decided to create their kinovselennuyu called Dark Universe , which will be based on the classic series Universal Monsters [136] and which will include all restarted movies from a series of classic monster movies, including " Frankenstein " , “ Dracula ”, “The Wolfman ”, “The Beast from the Black Lagoon ”, “The Invisible Man ”, “The Frankenstein Bride ” and “The Mummy ”. The further fate of the Dark Universe remains in doubt.
