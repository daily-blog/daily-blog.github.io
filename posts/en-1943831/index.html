" Game of Thrones " ( Engl.  The Game of Thrones ) - American television series in the genre of fantasy , based on a cycle of novels " A Song of Ice and Fire " by George RR Martin . Filmed under the supervision of David Benioff and D. B. Weiss for the HBO cable channel . Filming was conducted in several countries, including the United Kingdom ( Northern Ireland ), Malta , Croatia , Iceland and Morocco . The first season premiered in the USA on April 17, 2011., the last episode of the final season was shown on May 19, 2019 [3] [4] .

The action of the Game of Thrones takes place in a fictional world reminiscent of medieval Europe . In the series, many characters act simultaneously and several storylines develop . There are three main plot arches : the first is devoted to the struggle of several influential houses for the Iron Throne of the Seven Kingdoms or for independence from it; the second - to the descendant of the overthrown dynasty of rulers, the exiled princess who plans to return to the throne; the third - to the ancient brotherhood, protecting the state from threats from the north.

The series is characterized by the absence of strictly positive characters, many heroes commit ambiguous acts and may die on the screen unexpectedly for the viewer [5] . In the series there are fantastic creatures - dragons and undead . Especially for the series, artificial languages were developed : Dothraki , high Valyrian and several of its dialects.

The television series received high reviews from the viewers, becoming one of the largest budget projects on modern American television and the most expensive in the fantasy genre, despite the fact that many critics considered the amount of profanity, naturalistic murder scenes, erotic scenes and sexual hints excessive. Around the Game of Thrones an extensive fan community has sprung up . The series has been awarded many prestigious awards, including 38 prime time Emmy awards in various nominations, three Hugo awards for best performance , and Peabody Prizes . Peter Dinklidge , who performed the role of Tyrion Lannister , four times awardedEmmy Award for Best Supporting Actor and Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actor .

History of Creation

Background

The first proposal for the sale of film rights George Martin received 1-2 months after the release of the first novel of the series, " Game of Thrones " [26] ; suggestions continued to come as new books and their popularity grew. The commercial success of the film adaptations in the fantasy genre , especially the film ‚Äú The Lord of the Rings ‚Äù by Peter Jackson, also played its role [27] . Nevertheless, the writer for a long time was rather skeptical about the possible screen version of his works, considering them too long for film adaptation and too different from the standards of the television series [28] ; Martin Martin‚Äôs experience as a screenwriter and producer in the 1980s and 1990s contributed to this attitude [29].

In January 2007, the HBO cable channel acquired the rights to the screen version of the series [30] . The first and second draft scenarios of the pilot episode were presented in August 2007 [31] and in June 2008, respectively [32] and were approved by HBO [33] [34] . In September 2008, HBO secured the rights to the series, but the project was given the green light only in November [35] [36] . Almost immediately, a pilot synopsis appeared on the network, followed by the full script [37] .

Preparing for the shoot

The scriptwriters and executive producers of the project, in addition to Martin himself, were David Benioff and D. B. Weiss . It was assumed that they will be screenwriters of all episodes, except for one in each season, the script of which is written by Martin himself [30] [38] . However, in the future, Jane Aspenson and Brian Cogman joined the scriptwriting team , writing one script for the first season [39] .

Filming the pilot episode took almost a month, from October 24 [51] to November 19, 2009 [52] . On March 2, 2010, HBO confirmed that the first season would be removed. David Benioff and D. B. Weiss from the very beginning intended to film the entire series of ‚ÄúA Song of Ice and Fire‚Äù if HBO would give them such an opportunity. The budget of the second season, according to the producers, was 15% higher [141] . The production budget of the most expensive series - Chernovodnaya - amounted to 8 million US dollars. [142]

Languages

Although the series is American , the vast majority of the actors employed in it are subjects of Great Britain or Ireland . Many characters speak with accents characteristic of different areas of the British Isles [148] . The characters from Westeros speak British English , and their reprimand is often characteristic of the region of England, which geographically correlates with the corresponding region of Westeros. So, Eddard Stark, a native of the North, speaks with an accent characteristic of the north of England, while Southerner Tywin Lannister speaks with an accent typical of the inhabitants of the English south. Actors playing foreign characters speak with ‚Äúforeign‚Äù accents, real or simulated[149] . The inconsistency of this approach has been criticized by British viewers, including professional linguists: in the series, even members of the same family can speak different versions of English. Anthony Grant, professor of historical linguistics from the University of Edgehill , Named shown in the series set a hodgepodge of accents ( Eng.  A mish-mash ) and laid the blame on American producers [150] .

The series contains dialogues in artificial languages , foreign to Westeros - Dothraki and various dialects of Valyrian . The HBO channel hired the American linguist David Peterson [151] , who developed a vocabulary, phonetic and grammatical structure for each language [152] . According to Peterson, as of September 2011, there were over 3 thousand words in the pre-Thracian language [153] . Dialogues in the pre-Thracian and Valyrian languages ‚Äã‚Äãare accompanied by forced subtitles. According to the BBC , the number of people who heard these languages ‚Äã‚Äãwhile watching the series exceeds the number of people who heard Welsh ,Irish and Scottish Gaelic combined [154] .

Costumes

Michelle Clapton was a costume designer for the first five seasons before being replaced by April Ferry . [ 113 ] Clapton returned to the show as a costume designer of the seventh season. [ 114 ].

The costumes used in this series came as a result of inspiration from a number of sources, such as Japanese and Persian armor. The Dothraki dress is similar to the Bedouin one (it was made from a fish skin resembling a dragon's scale), and savages wear animal skins like Inuit. [ 115 ] The bone armor of the feral is made of mold, real bones, ropes and latex. Although the extras, which played wild and sentinels, usually wore hats (which is normal in a cold climate), the main characters did not. So the viewer could always distinguish them in a crowd of characters.

All used clothing is aged for two weeks, so it seems realistic on high-definition televisions [ 116 ].

About two dozen wigs are used for actresses. Made from human hair up to 61 cm long, they cost up to $ 7,000 each, are washed and styled like real hair. Putting a wig takes a lot of time: Emilia Clark, for example, takes about two hours to turn her dark hair into a platinum blonde with braids. Other actors, such as Jack Gleason and Sophie Turner, often undergo hair dyeing. For characters such as Daenerys (Clark) and her Dothraki, their hair, wigs and costumes are treated as if they had not been washed for several weeks [ 115 ].
